# AI for Marketers — 2026-07-12

OpenAI dropped GPT-5.6 and a workplace agent in the same week, Google is rewriting the ad-transparency rulebook, and LinkedIn shipped a full AI creative suite inside Campaign Manager — the tools are moving faster than most teams can evaluate them.

## Frontier Model Watch

### ⭐ [OpenAI launches GPT-5.6 family: Sol, Terra, and Luna](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6/)
OpenAI released three GPT-5.6 variants on July 9 — Sol (flagship), Terra (balanced), Luna (cost-efficient) — with a new "ultra" mode that orchestrates sub-agents for complex work. **Why it matters:** Sol powers ChatGPT Work (see below); marketers building on the API now have three price/performance tiers ($1–$5 per 1M input tokens) to match use cases from bulk content to agentic campaigns.

### [OpenAI introduces GPT-Live full-duplex voice](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-live/)

## Steal This Workflow

### [Multi-agent ad review: stop being the creative QA bottleneck](https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/code-review-tips)
Adapt PostHog's multi-agent review pattern to ad creative QA: set up two AI agents with different review briefs (one checks brand-voice compliance, the other checks claim accuracy and legal risk). Route ad drafts through both before human sign-off. Key rule: the agent that drafted the ad can't review it — use a different model or provider for each reviewer. Result: only ads that genuinely need human judgment reach your desk; the rest ship with confidence.
_Source: PostHog Newsletter (adapted for marketing) · Relevance: Direct_
